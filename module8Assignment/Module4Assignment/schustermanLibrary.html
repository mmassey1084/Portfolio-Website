Schusterman Library

Historical Context

The Schusterman Library is an integral part of the OU-Tulsa Schusterman Center, named after philanthropists George Kaiser and the Schusterman family. The library supports the academic mission of the OU-Tulsa campus, especially in fields related to health sciences, medicine, and public health.

Current Usage

The Schusterman Library provides a wide range of resources, including physical books, digital databases, academic journals, and study spaces. It is particularly geared toward students in the medical, public health, and allied health programs, but it also serves the wider OU-Tulsa community. The library offers research assistance, study rooms, and access to specialized collections that support advanced academic research.

Architectural Details

The library features a modern design, with large windows that allow natural light to create a conducive study environment. Inside, it offers quiet study areas, group meeting rooms, and technology-equipped workstations. The layout is designed to accommodate both individual and collaborative learning, making it a central academic hub on campus.
